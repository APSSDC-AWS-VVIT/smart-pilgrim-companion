# Travel Etiquette & Eco-Awareness

## 🛕 Tirumala

1. Follow the prescribed dress code and temple traditions.
2. Maintain silence and avoid loud conversations inside temple premises.
3. Respect queue systems and cooperate with temple staff.
4. Avoid carrying prohibited items such as alcohol, tobacco, and non-vegetarian food.
5. Use reusable water bottles instead of single-use plastics.
6. Dispose of waste only in designated bins.
7. Do not feed monkeys, deer, or other wildlife.
8. Conserve water while using bathing and accommodation facilities.
9. Avoid damaging trees, plants, or natural rock formations.
10. Support eco-friendly transportation such as TTD buses and shared transport.
11. Keep sacred water bodies clean.
12. Respect fellow pilgrims regardless of language, region, or background.

## 🛕 Srisailam

1. Wear modest and culturally appropriate attire.
2. Maintain cleanliness in temple and forest areas.
3. Do not throw plastic waste along ghat roads or viewpoints.
4. Respect the biodiversity of the Nallamala Forest.
5. Avoid making excessive noise that may disturb wildlife.
6. Stay on designated pathways and trekking routes.
7. Follow forest department and temple regulations.
8. Use reusable bags and bottles during travel.
9. Avoid polluting the Krishna River and Pathala Ganga.
10. Participate in responsible tourism practices.
11. Encourage fellow travelers to maintain cleanliness.
12. Preserve historical and spiritual sites for future generations.

## 🛕 Srikalahasti

1. Maintain reverence during Rahu–Ketu Puja and darshan.
2. Follow temple instructions and designated queues.
3. Wear clean and traditional clothing.
4. Avoid littering around the temple complex.
5. Keep the Swarnamukhi River surroundings clean.
6. Use eco-friendly travel accessories such as cloth bags.
7. Avoid writing on walls or damaging heritage structures.
8. Respect local customs and religious practices.
9. Minimize food waste and dispose of leftovers responsibly.
10. Reduce the use of plastic packaging and bottles.
11. Support local vendors who promote sustainable products.
12. Help preserve the temple's spiritual and cultural heritage.
